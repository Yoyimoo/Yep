Oh, the joys of cannibalism. So many benefits.

Sometimes, humans let dumb values stop them from taking biological realities seriously. Take pooping, for example: Most people think pooping is nasty, even though it’s something we do almost every day. Now, some scientists think cannibalism deserves another look.

Cannibalism naturally occurs in lots of insects, fish, and mammals. And despite our aversion to it as a “civilized” species, cannibalism serves a purpose. A team led by UC San Diego researcher Benjamin Van Allen reviewed research on cannibalism and came to a conclusion that cannibalism could help to limit disease spread in some populations.
